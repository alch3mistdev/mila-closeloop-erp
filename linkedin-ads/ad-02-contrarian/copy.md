## Hook Strategy
Positions against the specific gap in existing tooling for non-SAP sources, without directly attacking SAP or SIs. This is the core competitive positioning insight — SAP's migration tools work well for SAP-to-SAP, but most large manufacturers have heterogeneous source systems. The "2018 acquisition" and "AS/400 at the legacy plant" are composite framings of validated patterns that readers recognize as their own reality.

**Psychological triggers used:**
- **Contrarian framing** — Challenges an assumption the audience hasn't questioned
- **Specificity** — "Oracle instance from the 2018 acquisition" reads like insider knowledge
- **Problem reframing** — Shifts the problem from "we need better SAP tools" to "SAP tools aren't designed for this"
