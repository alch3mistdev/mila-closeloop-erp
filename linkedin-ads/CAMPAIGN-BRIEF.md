## Technical Specifications

### Single Image Ads
- **Primary Dimensions:** 1200×1200px (1:1 square) — works across desktop and mobile
- **Mobile-Optimized:** 1080×1350px (4:5 vertical) — recommended for mobile campaigns, takes up maximum feed real estate
- **File Format:** PNG or JPG (export from SVG sources)
- **Max File Size:** 5 MB
- **Introductory Text:** 150 characters before "...see more" truncation (600 max desktop)
- **Headline:** 70 characters recommended (200 max)
- **Description:** 70 characters max

### Carousel Ads
- **Card Dimensions:** 1080×1080px (1:1 square, required)
- **Number of Cards:** 3
- **Max File Size:** 10 MB per card
- **Introductory Text:** 150 characters recommended (255 max)
- **Card Headline:** 45 characters max

### Text Ads
- **Image:** 100×100px (JPG or PNG)
- **Headline:** 25 characters max
- **Description:** 75 characters max

## Strategic Recommendations (Based on Q4 2025 / Q1 2026 Research)

### 1. Deploy as Thought Leader Ads Where Possible
Thought Leader Ads (sponsored posts from the founder's personal profile) deliver **3–5× higher CTR at roughly half the CPC** of standard sponsored content. Ads 1, 3, 4, and 5 are ideal candidates for TLA reformatting — they read as authentic practitioner observations, not corporate marketing.

### 2. Use 4:5 Vertical Format for Mobile Campaigns
The 4:5 ratio (1080×1350) takes up maximum feed real estate on mobile. Create vertical variants of all single-image creatives for mobile-targeted campaigns.

### 3. A/B Test Aggressively
Only ~25% of LinkedIn ad creatives convert at all. Test 2–4 headline/copy variations per ad concept. The SVGs provided are starting designs — iterate based on performance data.

### 4. Full-Funnel Architecture
- **60% budget** → Top-of-funnel (Ads 2, 3, 5): Awareness, problem education
- **25% budget** → Mid-funnel (Ads 1, 4, 6, 7): Lead capture (checklist downloads)
- **15% budget** → Bottom-of-funnel: Retarget checklist downloaders with diagnostic offer

### 5. Creative Contrast Against LinkedIn's Feed
LinkedIn's feed is predominantly white/light gray with blue accents. CloseLoop's **warm orange (#E84E1B) and dark teal (#005E66)** create strong contrast. This is a significant brand advantage — lean into it.

### 6. Refresh Cadence
Swap creatives every 4–6 weeks to combat ad fatigue. The 7 variants provide enough rotation for an initial 12-week campaign cycle.
