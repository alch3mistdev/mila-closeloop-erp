## Hook Strategy
Composite anecdote drawn from multiple research interviews. This is the most emotionally provocative ad in the set — designed to be visceral, memorable, and shareable. "A Dead Employee's ID" creates an immediate pattern interrupt. The story is specific enough to feel real and general enough that multiple organizations will recognize the pattern.

**Psychological triggers used:**
- **Pattern interrupt** — "Dead Employee's ID" breaks expected LinkedIn content patterns
- **Narrative transportation** — Story format bypasses analytical resistance
- **Visceral specificity** — Concrete enough to be memorable, not generic
- **Implied self-assessment** — Reader asks "could this be happening at my organization?"
- **Fear of the unknown** — "They become permanent" implies hidden, growing risk

**Note:** This ad has the highest viral potential. It's the most likely to be shared organically. Consider deploying as a Thought Leader Ad first to maximize organic amplification.

## Creative Brief
- **Background:** Very dark (#151311, near-black) — dramatic, almost ominous
- **Visual:** Stylized ID badge/credential card, faded and ghosted, with an "EXPIRED" overlay in deep red (#A52323). Abstract silhouette. The badge should look like it's fading away but still present in the system.
- **Typography:** White serif headline text with high contrast. "EXPIRED" stamp in red is the visual anchor.
- **Brand:** CloseLoop logo mark + wordmark in white, bottom
- **Mood:** Unsettling, memorable, story-driven. This should stop the scroll.
