## Hook Strategy
References validated cost and timeline data from a former plant finance leader interview ("hundreds of millions" over 5–10 years). The headline uses numbers as psychological hooks — "nine figures" and "a decade" are concrete enough to be credible but rounded enough to protect source anonymity. The stat-driven approach builds authority while the "Still Not Done" kicker creates emotional weight.

**Psychological triggers used:**
- **Concrete numbers** — "$100M+" and "10+ years" are immediately graspable and startling
- **Authority** — "first-hand accounts from plant finance leaders" establishes research credibility
- **Loss aversion** — "Still Not Done" implies ongoing waste and risk
- **Contrast** — "months → weeks" compression promise creates desire
