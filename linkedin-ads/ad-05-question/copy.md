## Hook Strategy
Provokes immediate self-assessment. The three rapid-fire questions are designed so that the honest answer for most post-migration organizations is "no" — which creates cognitive dissonance. Plant controllers who have lived through inconsistent reporting will recognize themselves immediately. The question format forces the brain to attempt an answer, creating engagement before the reader can scroll past.

**Psychological triggers used:**
- **Question-based engagement** — The brain automatically tries to answer questions
- **Serial questioning** — Three questions in rapid succession create mounting pressure
- **Self-assessment** — Reader evaluates their own situation, making it personal
- **Cognitive dissonance** — If the answer is "no," the reader feels compelled to act
- **Implied peer comparison** — "Can YOU" suggests others have already asked this question
